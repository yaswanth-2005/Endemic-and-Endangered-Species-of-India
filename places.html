Western Ghats

The Western Ghats, also known as the Sahyadri mountain range, is a mountain range that runs parallel to the western coast of India. The range stretches for 1,600 km (990 mi) and covers an area of 160,000 km2 (62,000 sq mi). The Ghats run through the states of Gujarat, Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu. .

North.,
East India

Northeast India is very rich in faunal diversity. There are as many as 15 species of non-human primates and most important of them are hoolock gibbon, stumptied macaque, pigtailed macaque, golden langur, hanuman langur and rhesus monkey. The most important and endangered species is one-horned rhinoceros.

  • One-horned Rhinoceros
